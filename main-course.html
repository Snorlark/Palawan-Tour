Main Course
Appetizing Main Course: Delightful Starters from Palawan Start your culinary journey through Palawan with these appetizing Main Course that offer a taste of the region's unique flavors and ingredients. Whether you're looking for a bold culinary adventure or a comforting classic, these Palawan delicacies are sure to tantalize your taste buds and leave you craving for more.

Lato Salad

Coron

Lato Salad is a refreshing dish featuring sea grapes, a type of green algae known for its burst of ocean flavor. Combined with tomatoes and onions, and dressed in a tangy vinegar mixture with a touch of sugar, this salad offers a delightful blend of freshness and umami.

Nido Soup

El Nido

Nido Soup, also known as bird’s nest soup, is a luxurious delicacy made from the nests of swiftlets. This rich, savory soup is simmered with chicken broth, ginger, and garlic, thickened with cornstarch, and finished with beaten egg whites and a dash of sesame oil for a silky texture.

Adobong Pusit

Taytay

Adobong Pusit is a squid dish cooked in the traditional adobo style with vinegar and soy sauce. The squid is sautéed with garlic, onions, and tomatoes, then simmered with bay leaves and black pepper for a savory and slightly tangy flavor. It’s a delicious main course that pairs perfectly with steamed rice.

Lechon Kawali

Coron

Lechon Kawali is a popular Filipino dish featuring deep-fried pork belly. The pork is boiled with garlic, peppercorns, and bay leaves until tender, then deep-fried until crispy. Served with a side of liver sauce or vinegar dipping sauce, this dish is a crispy, savory delight.

Chicken Inasal

Roxas

Chicken Inasal is a flavorful grilled chicken dish marinated in a mixture of calamansi juice, soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, ginger, and lemongrass. The chicken is grilled over hot coals, basted with annatto oil for a vibrant color and smoky flavor, and served with rice and dipping sauces.

Pala-Pala Grilled Fish

Puerto Princesa

Pala-Pala Grilled Fish is a staple in Puerto Princesa, featuring freshly caught fish marinated in calamansi juice, soy sauce, salt, and pepper, then grilled to perfection. Served with a side of tomatoes and onions, this dish offers a fresh, smoky flavor that captures the essence of Palawan’s coastal cuisine.
